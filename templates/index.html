Quantifying Carbon Stock: the Roseburn Path

  • Resources

Trees, trams, change

In February 2024, the City of Edinburgh Council proposed an extension to the existing tram network.
Much attention has since been drawn to the Roseburn Path – the favoured tramline would replace this ecological corridor and green space.

Interested in the potential loss of trees, a team from the University of Edinburgh sought to quantify the carbon stock of the path.
Adopting a ‘ground up’ approach, this first required estimating biomass at the individual tree level; the map below shows a subset of the trees sampled. A considerable range of species can be observed, with each map icon providing more information.

To use this map, click on any marker of your choice. For viewing of specific tree species, filtering is available in the top right corner.
For viewing of specific biomass ranges, filtering is available in the top centre of the map.
You can use the layers tab in the top right to view in light map mode or satellite map mode.

Using this field data, biomass estimations were upscaled to the entirety of the path. (For information around methodology, please refer to the formal report produced). It is estimated the path contains 1,469,366kg of biomass, equivalent to around 734,683kg of carbon.

These findings provide a valuable first insight. However, there is uncertainty to these results (found in the formal report).

Estimations should also be placed in the broader context of reduced CO2 emissions from car travel. The team’s preliminary calculations suggest that around 3.07 million tram journeys (in place of car journeys) would negate this 735,000kg of carbon stock loss. For reference, around 13.5 million trips could be made on the new tram route every year.
